WILDLIFE CONSERVATION REPORT 2026: TIGER RECOVERY AND METRICS
Subject: Bengal Tiger (Panthera tigris tigris) population assessment in Sector-A.
Date: July 2026

1. POPULATION ESTIMATES:
Through intensive camera-trapping and footprint analysis over the last 12 months, we have documented a total of 48 individual tigers in Sector-A. This represents a significant 18% increase from the 41 individuals recorded during the previous annual census. Cubs and sub-adult sightings have also increased, confirming a healthy reproduction and recovery rate.

2. THREAT AND POACHING REDUCTION:
Thanks to the deployment of new anti-poaching units, patrol coverage has expanded by 40%. The rangers registered a total of zero poaching incidents or trap recoveries in Sector-A in the past six months, down from three incidents in the previous cycle. Illegal human incursions have decreased by 85% due to solar-powered fencing.

3. CONCLUSION:
The ecological indicators confirm that the Bengal Tiger population is actively recovering and thriving in Sector-A. The ecological target goal has been fully met, with a high confidence score.
